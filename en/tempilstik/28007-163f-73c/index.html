Product Description

Tempilstik 163°F — Applications & Standards

Verify preheat temperature for structural steel. Prevents cold cracking / hydrogen-induced cracking.

No power source and no periodic calibration required. When the crayon mark melts on the metal surface, the temperature has reached 163°F (73°C) — a clear visual result that can be photographed for QA/QC records right on site.

Technical Knowledge

Standards-based Usage Context

📐 Applicable Standard

AWS D1.1 Table 3.2 — standard preheat for low- to medium-carbon structural steel.

Steel A36, SS400, A572 Gr50. Thickness 20–50 mm.

See the full AWS D1.1 preheat temperature guide tempil.vn

📖 How to Use

Correct field measurement technique

Mark the crayon on the steel surface before heating, at least 75 mm (3 in) away from the weld joint per AWS D1.1 Section 5. When the temperature reaches 163°F the mark melts — the signal that the next step may proceed. Do not measure directly on a surface just heated by an open flame.

Full Tempilstik usage guide tempil.vn

⚖️ Equipment Comparison

Why not use an IR laser thermometer?

IR laser thermometers are affected by emissivity — causing 5–15% error on steel with mill scale, rust or primer. Tempilstik works by a chemical mechanism, independent of surface condition. In outdoor conditions (wind, rain) on O&G sites, Tempilstik gives more reliable results.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ Documents & Standards

Is Tempilstik 163°F accepted in a WPS?

Yes. AWS D1.1, ASME Section IX and API 1104 all accept temperature-indicating crayons such as Tempilstik as a valid temperature-measurement method. Tempilstik comes with a full Certificate of Origin, Mill Certificate and Technical Data Sheet for your project material file.

See the full Tempilstik FAQ tempil.vn

❓ Storage

Shelf life and storage conditions

Tempilstik has no official expiry date when stored correctly: in a dry, cool place out of direct sunlight and away from high ambient heat. The chemical composition stays stable over time with proper storage.
